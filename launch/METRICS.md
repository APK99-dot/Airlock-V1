# Metrics tracker — Airlock launch

Vanity metrics lie. Track leading signals that predict the outcome you actually
want (inbound interest + credibility with decision-makers), not just applause.

## Targets (first 14 days)

| Metric | Floor (baseline) | Target (good) | Stretch (viral) | What it signals |
|--------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| ⭐ GitHub stars | 40 | 150 | 500+ | The concept resonates with builders — proof the problem is felt |
| 🍴 Forks | 3 | 15 | 50+ | People intend to *use* it, not just bookmark it — strongest OSS signal |
| 💬 Qualified comments¹ | 8 | 25 | 60+ | You reached practitioners, not just impressions |
| 📥 Inbound DMs² | 2 | 8 | 20+ | The career/consulting outcome — the real point |
| 👀 Post 2 impressions | 3k | 15k | 60k+ | Reach (weakest signal — necessary, not sufficient) |
| 🐛 Issues/PRs from strangers | 0 | 3 | 10+ | Community forming — the difference between a repo and a project |

¹ **Qualified comment** = names a specific use case, failure, or technical point ("we'd guard our internal refund tool with this") — NOT "🔥 great work." Count only qualified ones.
² **Inbound DM** = someone starts a conversation *because of* the post: a hiring lead, a "can this handle X," a podcast/collab ask, a "we're piloting agents" from a decision-maker.

## What each outcome tells you to do next
- **Stars high, DMs low** → the tech landed but the *positioning* for decision-makers didn't. Reframe Post 3 around business risk/ROI, not internals.
- **DMs high, stars low** → your network values *you* more than the artifact reached *its* audience. Ask a well-networked contact to reshare; post in 1–2 relevant communities.
- **Comments high, forks low** → interesting to talk about, friction to adopt. Cut the quickstart to 3 commands; add a one-click Colab/replit.
- **Everything low** → likely a distribution problem, not a product one. Re-cut the demo (thumbnail = a red BLOCKED frame), repost at a better time, seed it in one high-signal community first.
